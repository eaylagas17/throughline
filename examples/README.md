# examples

Worked before/afters for the two pains throughline exists to fix. Each folder pairs
the artifact you'd leave behind *without* throughline (`before.md`) against the
`.throughline/NNNN.md` item throughline captures instead (`after.md`), with a short
walkthrough that narrates the two fresh-session outcomes.

| Example | Scenario | What it shows |
|---|---|---|
| [`parked-idea/`](./parked-idea) | Park an idea, resume it cold weeks later | Capture fidelity: the *why* and the constraints survive, so a fresh Ship needs no re-explaining and asks only the genuinely open question. |
| [`phase-handoff/`](./phase-handoff) | Hand Phase 2 to a fresh session (the hero) | A prose paste-prompt drifts; a throughline checkpoint re-validates against the plan and catches the drift at the door. |

## How to read these

Open a folder's `README.md` first, then look at `before.md` and `after.md` as the raw
artifacts they represent. The phase-handoff example also ships the `plan.md` its item
anchors to, so the re-validate step has a real source of truth to check against.

These are illustrative walkthroughs, not captures from a live repo. The *quantitative*
proof (a fresh session's plan-adherence with vs. without throughline, scored on a fixed
rubric) is the job of the forthcoming handoff-fidelity benchmark.
